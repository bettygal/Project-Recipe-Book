Amala & Ewedu

Amala & ewedu is one of the most favored of current trending foods in the world . It’s appreciated by millions every day.
It’s simple, it’s quick, it tastes yummy. They are fine and they look fantastic. Amala and ewedu is something that I have loved my whole life.

To get started with this particular recipe, we must first prepare a few components. You can cook amala & ewedu using 6 ingredients and 4 steps. Here is how you cook that.

The ingredients needed to make Amala & ewedu:
Amala & Ewedu Recipes Quantity
yam flour 2 cups
Ewedu leaf 50 grams
Potash 1 pinch
Salt 1 pinch
Locust beans 1 Tsp
Goat meat stew Enough
Steps to make Amala and ewedu:
  1. Put water on the fire and bring to boil, lower the heat and add yam flour whilst stiring using a kitchen spatula until you get your desired consistency. Add a little hot water and cover the pot, allow to cook for 3mins, stir again and turn off the heat. Pack into clean nylon and put in a warmer.
  2. Pick your ewedu leaf, try avoiding the stem, wash well and drain. Put a little water on fire, add potash, allow to boil and add ewedu leaf. Allow to cook until soft, remove from heat and blend. Put your pot back on fire, add a little water, add locust beans and salt, allow to boil, then add your blended ewedu. Lower the heat, cook for 1min and turn off the heat.
  3. Goat meat stew to be used
  4. It's ready serve hot and enjoy.

Ewa Agoyin

This is a street food that you can find especially from the vendors in Lagos. It’s hearty, full of flavor, and spicy. It uses chilis and hot red peppers, as well as onions to create the spicy taste. At its center are beans which have been boiled until tender enough to be mashed.

Ingredients for Making Ewa Agoyin include:
Ewa Agoyin Recipes Quantity
Beans 1 kg
Palm Oil 4 cksp
Crushed Ginger 2 Tsp
Chilli Pepper 3 Tsp
Chopped Onions 1 big bulb
Seasoning Cubes 2
Salt half Tsp
Cooking Instructions

Remove stone and dirt from beans,cook,wheñ half done,add seasoning cubes, taste to adjust with salt,mash with a wooden spatula till sticky and runny,cover,when it boils and dries,check if it's done,you may add lil more water till soft,remove from heat,heat oil in a sauce pan,bleach slightly,add pepper, ginger on low heat,stir fry for 2mins,add onions,fry for 2mins till crunchy,remove from heat,put beans on a plate,top with sauce enjoy!!!

Pounded yam with Egusi soup

Pounded yam is, as you may have guessed, a staple in the Nigerian kitchen, eaten together with many other dishes on this list. It is somewhat similar to mashed potatoes, but its consistency is far doughier. Like fufu, it is considered a swallow dish; as in, you cut out a piece of the pounded yam, press an indentation on it with your thumb, and then like a spoon you use it to scoop up some of the stew before swallowing the bite as a whole.

Ingredients for making Pounded yam with Egusi soup:
Ingredients Quantity
Medium yam 1
Water/td> 1 bown
Salp 1 tsp
Grounded melon 1 tin
pepper sauce 4 SP
Palm oil 2 SP
Sliced vegetables 2 small bunches
Grinded crayfish half tin
Sliced Pkomo 10 pieces
Seasoning cubes 3
Cooking Instructions
  1. Peel the yam, slice and wash and cook with water and salt till completely soft.
  2. Using a mortar and pestle pound the yam till soft and sticky, sprinkling the yam water at intervals
  3. Mould into desired shape and size..... Keep aside
  4. For the soup
  5. Mix the egusi with water to form a paste
  6. Add the oil into the pot and fry the grinded pepper mix for 5mins, scoop out, add little oil and add the egusi mix gradually in ball
  7. Fry for 3 mins, add the fried pepper mix and water, salt, pkomo and seasoning
  8. Allow to cook for 30 mins, add in the cray fish cook for 5 more mins then finally add ginger/garlic paste and sliced spinach and spring onion
  9. Allow it to steam for 5 mins....ready to serve for lunch or dinner
